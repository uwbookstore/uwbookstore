Incoming Student FAQ

University Book Store receives information regarding textbook requirements from your professors as early as months before the semester starts through the first weeks of classes. We make every effort to get your books on the shelf as soon as we learn of the requirements. If you don't find a book, you can keep checking back or you can go to the information counter in the course materials department to inquire on the status of your book.

Textbooks are located on the lower level of University Book Store at 711 State Street. Class books are arranged first by department, next by class within each department. You should bring a list of your classes with you when you shop for your books each semester. This list should contain the names of your professors along with lecture and section numbers. This will help you make sure you're buying the right books for the right classes. It's not uncommon to have three professors teaching a particular class using three completely different sets of books.

You may have your books delivered to your home or dorm, or you may pick them up on the lower level of University Book Store, 711 State Street, during regular Book Store hours. You must present a valid picture I.D. at time of pickup.

Don't worry! We can get additional copies. Order the book through our website and you'll be notified when it's available. The book will initially appear as backordered on your order but will be fulfilled as soon as we receive additional stock.

Yes. You may find information about buyback at text.uwbookstore.com/Buyback.

Buyback prices are dynamic and can change daily based on need. We buy books for store use and on behalf of our wholesale partners.

Yes! We rent many required and recommended books. If available, you will see a rental option listed below the purchasing options for the title. The rental due date and rental agreement can be found under the book notes on the same page. The due date is always the last day of finals for the semester and you'll receive several emails toward the end of the term reminding you of this. Return your rented book to the Course Materials Dept (lower level).

Some instructors provide books lists to other local shops. We may very well have used copies of the books you need. Bring us a copy of your syllabus after you have gone to your first class and we will do all we can to find used copies of the books you need.

The University Book Store accepts cash, check, Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover and Wiscard, the University of Wisconsin-Madison's debit card.

You can put money into your Wiscard Campus Cash account at the Memorial Union Cashier's Office or online.

The average student spends $350-$450 per semester at the University Book Store.
